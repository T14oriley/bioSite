Marvel Studios is one of the largest, and most popular media company, on the planet right now. Superhero movies seem to have the world in a choke hold with the many story lines and plots being developed. Disney, being the major financial backer of Marvel Studio has seen to the idea of superheros being the most sought after pop culture product on the market. When it comes to film making, many have their eyes on the media giant.

Gentry entered this scene working as a Personal assistant on the movie "The Tomorrow War" Starring Chris Pratt. They handled most office affairs and assisted on set where materials and equipment were needed. They enjoyed the experience but did admit it was a stressful environment with the amount of demands and time constraints that were in place.

The film industry is known to be one of the most brutal and rewarding experiences many aspiring film makers can work towards. The tales Gentry has told me about the industry hold true to that statement and also solidify my thoughts that I would never want to attempt getting into the movie scene.

When they first arrived in Atlanta, Gentry moved in with a handful of friends who shared the same dreams of cinematography at Ball State University. They all shared a house and helped each other find opportunities where they could. Success was found in assisting with music videos for various artists and working side projects for those willing to pay. To make ends meet, Gentry worked a side job as a delivery driver with a local chinese styled restaurant.

About a year in their adventure was where opportunities began and the traction for success started rolling.

After their first major film adventure, an inside connection got Gentry in touch to work as a PA on set for the Marvel films. The two major films they got to work on and had their name in the credits were "Spideman: No Way Home" and "Guardians of the Galaxy: Volume 3".

These experiences would end up being some of the most exciting yet dreadful work they had taken part of. Seeing the cast and being able to take part on the films were a treat in themselves but these benefits did not match the employment endured. The industry is known for being brutal towards it's employees and this was shown in Gentry's work detail. Benefits were lacking and did little to accommodate most medical needs. The pay was way lower than most would expect and many hours of overtime were required with little extra pay. In general Personal Assistants are heavily mistreated and this was one of the major factors that lead to Gentry moving home to Indiana.

Family was here and a bride to be was waiting for them. They enjoyed their time working on major film, but unfortunately work conditions made this dream unbearable.
